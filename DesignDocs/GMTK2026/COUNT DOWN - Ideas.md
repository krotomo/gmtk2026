Scratch area:

Alright, looks like mark brown already anticipated the various funny ways people could interpret this one, so that took the wind out of my sails a bit, but that doesn't mean those avenues aren't still available. Obviously this lends itself to timers, counters, urgency. If taken as a definitional theme, it should feel frantic to play our game. However, given that we will be given time to explain/justify our connection to the theme, as well as the fact that games will not be disqualified if they fail to adhere to it, it almost leads me to believe that the theme doesn't matter.

No dice on that front, I'm too competitive to just dispense with it. Gotta come up with something that's incisive, something that *directly* adheres to the theme, preferably in a non-obvious way. Would be nice to get that feeling of dawning realization and a chuckle out of people when they get it. Need to keep scope reined in, so can't go too crazy, one core mechanic to work around would be best, but it's better to design big and then cut than to take too much time trying to come up with something totally novel. Let's see what I've got in me

Brainstorming:
Concept 1: 2d top-down/isometric assassination game. The game is set in a mansion/garden/restaurant, something upscale. The player plays as a janitor/waiter, something low-class/blue collar. The player's goal is to eliminate as many members of the nobility as possible within a timer, without being detected (think hitman), but the round will only be considered a success if you've killed the primary target: The Count

Concept 2: 2d space game, structured a bit like an endless runner. The player controls a spaceship as it is hurtling through space. A timer is ticking down at the top of the screen. The player's goal is to use the (depending on speed) planets/star systems/galaxies/black holes they pass by to give themselves a gravity assist/speed boost to continually increase their speed. Because of relativity, the faster they go, the slower the timer ticks down. The aim is to make it as far as possible (maybe we set some goal destination, but continue to score beyond it) within the time limit. Limited player controls, maybe even just adjustments to left/right direction.

Concept 3: 2d post-apocalyptic. The outside world isn't safe, you'll begin to die once exposed. The player starts with a bunker with some number of people in it. The goal is to send one person out, explore the surrounding region, gather resources, and get them back to the bunker before the environment kills them. The more people in the bunker, the more resources needed. If the player comes back with an excess of resources, new people can be born/added to the population (maybe even include recovering survivors from outside somehow?). If there aren't enough resources, the population is reduced. The game ends when if the last person from the bunker returns alone. Could include procedural generation for the map for variety, or make it fixed. Challenge comes from needing to spend part of each consecutive attempt creating new paths to go further more quickly for future runs.

Concept 4: 2d exploration/adventure. You play as a diver. Unfortunately, you suck. You decided to go diving into caves with no practice or equipment, save for a light. The player will control the diver and try to navigate them, with their limited supply of breath, from one air pocket (which have limited uses!) to another through an underwater cave system. There will be some obstacles/hazards/puzzles to consider and overcome, as well as false paths. If the player drowns, we keep track of where they died, and on the next run the old body becomes a new obstacle. Goal is to make it as far as possible (to the end?) before dying.

Concept 5: 2d gambling. You have debt, a lot of debt, you also have no job, no skills, and no time. With a hundred bucks to your name, you hit the casino with a dream. We could support slots/blackjack pretty easily, would be best to add ways for the player to tweak the games somehow as they go. (Various forms of "cheats" unlockable via winnings?) Goal is to win enough money to pay off the debt without getting kicked out of the casino.

Concept 6: 2d rhythm game? (almost definitely impossible in the time frame, but whatever). Could target mechanics similar to rhythm doctor, deal specifically with keeping rhythm rather than incorporating any elements of melody or harmony. Maybe we don't make it specifically "musical" in theme, only in practice. Maybe you play as a missile commander receiving orders to launch at specific targets, and you're scored on timing/accuracy.

Concept 7: 2d incremental game. You have aspirations toward scrooge mcduck wealth, a literal money pit. You've gotten it started, but you need a lot more money. Typical incremental gameplay loop, upgrades to income with scaling cost, larger amounts of money unlock better ways of earning money, but be careful! The more money you earn, the larger the hole gets, the faster it disappears into the pit, and once it's in the pit it can no longer be spent. Goal is to earn enough money to fill the hole completely without running out of usable funds partway.
